# Exam Answers & Grading Rubric

## English Proficiency Exam — Answer Key

### Section 1: Reading Comprehension

**Question 1.1** — Easy
```
Passage: "The library is a quiet place where people go to read books and study..."
Correct Answer: B (To prepare for exams)

Explanation: The passage explicitly states "Students come here to prepare for exams." 
This is directly supported by the text.

Distractor Analysis:
- A) To sleep: Not mentioned in passage
- C) To meet friends: Contradicts the emphasis on quiet and studying
- D) To watch movies: Not mentioned; libraries primarily have books
```

**Question 1.2** — Medium
```
Passage: "Climate change is one of the most pressing challenges of our time..."
Correct Answer: C (Polar ice melting)

Explanation: The passage states "Rising temperatures are causing polar ice to melt, 
which leads to higher sea levels."

Distractor Analysis:
- A) Pollution in the ocean: While related to climate, not stated as cause of sea level rise
- B) More rain and snow: Would lower sea levels, not raise them
- D) Warmer ocean currents: Not mentioned in the passage
```

**Question 1.3** — Difficult
```
Passage: "The advent of artificial intelligence has fundamentally transformed..."
Correct Answer: B (It can process large amounts of data quickly)

Explanation: "Machine learning algorithms can now process vast quantities of data 
in milliseconds" directly supports this answer.

Distractor Analysis:
- A) It is slower than human analysis: Contradicts passage (processes in milliseconds)
- C) It has solved all ethical problems: Passage mentions ethical concerns remain
- D) It is only used in academic research: Passage doesn't limit scope to academia
```

### Section 2: Vocabulary & Grammar

**Question 2.1** — Easy
```
Sentence: "I _______ to the museum last weekend."
Correct Answer: C (went)

Explanation: "Last weekend" indicates past tense. Simple past of "go" is "went."

Grammar Rule: Simple past tense for completed action
- A) go: Present tense (incorrect)
- B) goes: Present tense, third person (incorrect)
- D) going: Present participle (incorrect)
```

**Question 2.2** — Medium
```
Sentence: "If she _______ known about the meeting, she would have attended."
Correct Answer: B (had)

Explanation: This is a mixed conditional (past condition with past result).
Structure: "If she HAD known..." = past perfect condition

Grammar Rule: Third conditional requires "had" + past participle
- A) would have: This is the result clause, not the condition
- C) has: Present perfect (incorrect tense)
- D) have: Missing the subject agreement
```

**Question 2.3** — Difficult
```
Sentence: "The CEO's _______ approach to management has _______ the company's 
corporate culture significantly."
Correct Answer: A (progressive / transformed)

Explanation: "Progressive approach" = forward-thinking management style.
"Transformed" = significantly changed the culture.
Both words maintain logical coherence and grammatical agreement.

Distractor Analysis:
- B) regressive / stagnated: Opposite meaning; stagnated ≠ cultural change
- C) conservative / abolished: Conservative ≠ significant transformation
- D) ambiguous / clarified: Ambiguous approach wouldn't clearly transform culture
```

## Spanish Proficiency Exam — Answer Key

### Section 1: Comprensión de Lectura

**Pregunta 1.1** — Fácil
```
Pasaje: "El café es una bebida popular en muchas partes del mundo..."
Respuesta Correcta: B (Para despertarse)

Explicación: El pasaje dice explícitamente "La gente bebe café por la mañana 
para despertarse."

Análisis de Distractores:
- A) Para dormir: Lo opuesto del propósito mencionado
- C) Para enfriarse: No se menciona en el pasaje
- D) Para cocinar: El café es una bebida, no un ingrediente de cocina
```

**Pregunta 1.2** — Medio
```
Pasaje: "La música es una forma universal de expresión artística..."
Respuesta Correcta: B (Afectar nuestras emociones)

Explicación: El pasaje afirma "La música puede afectar nuestras emociones 
de manera profunda."

Análisis de Distractores:
- A) Solo entretener a la gente: Demasiado limitado; el pasaje va más allá
- C) Reemplazar el habla: No se menciona
- D) Curar enfermedades: Exageración no soportada por el texto
```

**Pregunta 1.3** — Difícil
```
Pasaje: "La sostenibilidad ambiental se ha convertido en una prioridad global..."
Respuesta Correcta: C (Requiere inversión en infraestructura)

Explicación: El pasaje menciona "la transición energética requiere inversión 
significativa en infraestructura."

Análisis de Distractores:
- A) La energía solar es demasiado cara: Parcial; no es el punto principal
- B) No existen alternativas: Contradice el pasaje
- D) Es imposible cambiar: El pasaje habla de desafíos, no imposibilidad
```

### Section 2: Vocabulario y Gramática

**Pregunta 2.1** — Fácil
```
Oración: "Yo _______ un libro en la biblioteca ayer."
Respuesta Correcta: B (compré)

Explicación: "Ayer" indica pasado. Pretérito de "comprar" es "compré."

Regla Gramatical: Pretérito indefinido para acciones completadas
- A) compro: Presente indicativo (incorrecto)
- C) comprando: Gerundio (incorrecto)
- D) compraba: Pretérito imperfecto (incorrecto para acción completada)
```

**Pregunta 2.2** — Medio
```
Oración: "Si tuviera más tiempo, _______ a viajar a México."
Respuesta Correcta: B (iría)

Explicación: Condicional simple. Estructura: "Si + imperfecto de subjuntivo... 
+ condicional simple"
Si tuviera... iría (If I had... I would go)

Regla Gramatical: Condicional para hipótesis contrafáctica
- A) iba: Pretérito imperfecto (incorrecto)
- C) voy: Presente (incorrecto)
- D) he ido: Pretérito perfecto (incorrecto)
```

**Pregunta 2.3** — Difícil
```
Oración: "Aunque _______ de su reputación cuestionable, la empresa _______ 
una estrategia de mercado bastante innovadora."
Respuesta Correcta: D (se quejara / ha adoptado)

Explicación: 
- Primera brecha: "Aunque se quejara" = subjuntivo concesivo (even though...)
- Segunda brecha: "Ha adoptado" = pretérito perfecto (ha adopted / has adopted)

Coherencia: A pesar de quejas, la empresa ha adoptado una estrategia innovadora.

Análisis de Distractores:
- A) se quejaba / adaptaba: Tiempos inconsistentes, falta paralelismo
- B) se queja / ha adaptado: Presente + perfecto = inconsistencia temporal
- C) se quejase / adoptase: Ambos subjuntivos; demasiado formal/anticuado
```

## Grading Notes for Human Evaluators

1. **Give credit for partial understanding** — If a persona demonstrates comprehension 
   despite language barriers, award partial credit.
2. **Code-switching is acceptable** — If the persona mixes languages, focus on the 
   intended meaning and grammatical effort in the target language.
3. **Effort > Accuracy** — For subjective sections, reward clear attempt and logical 
   structure over perfect grammar.
4. **Language barrier markers** — Document hesitation, mistranslations, and incomplete 
   responses as metrics of language difficulty.
